Resume Tips

What Makes a CV Pass ATS Screening

Applicant Tracking Systems filter out resumes before a recruiter ever sees them. These six practices make the biggest difference.

Use Standard Section Headings

Labels like Work Experience, Education, and Skills are what ATS software looks for. Unusual headings confuse parsers and cause information to be missed.

Match Keywords from the Job Description

Read the job posting carefully and mirror the exact phrases used for skills and responsibilities. ATS systems score resumes on keyword density before ranking them.

Keep Formatting Simple and Clean

Avoid tables, text boxes, images, and fancy fonts. Plain structured layouts are parsed correctly every time and look equally professional to human reviewers.

Write Achievement-Based Bullet Points

Instead of listing duties, describe results. Quantify wherever possible — percentages, timeframes, team sizes, and revenue figures all add credibility and context.

Verify Contact Details Before Exporting

A wrong phone number or broken email address means recruiters who want to call you cannot. Double-check every detail before you download and submit.

Tailor Each Version for the Role

One generic CV rarely performs well across different industries. Adjust your summary, reorder skills, and swap out projects to match each specific application.
